## 1. Giorgos's EB issuance rule — validated by Will, actionable for us

**The discussion** (low-traffic scenario, Giorgos ↔ Will):

- Setup: high-priority traffic ≈ |RB|/2, low-priority slightly below.
- Will's concern: plain Praos serves everything with no delay there, while Leios
  delays the low-priority half behind EB certification — "strictly worse".
- Giorgos's counter: that comparison spends RB space the mechanism reserves for
  price recalculation (the half-full target — the second half of the RB is the
  controller's headroom). Against **Praos + dynamic pricing**, Praos serves only
  the high-priority half too.
- **Giorgos's rule: only issue an EB if it is at least |RB|/2 full.** Same
  service in the low-traffic case, and (his 2nd message) comparing Praos w/o DP
  vs Leios w/o DP, the rule makes Leios's latency better *always* — natural with
  or without DP.
- **Will (thread): "Based on an experiment I've thrown together, your solution
  does actually look quite good. It seems better than my mixed-RB-mode
  suggestion actually."** → the rule is the current team direction.

**Proposed prototype update (P1): implement the EB min-fill rule.**
`selectForForge` (consensus `Mempool/Lanes.hs`) — do not produce an endorser
block when the optimistic selection carries fewer than `|RB|/2` bytes
(45,056 B); the txs simply stay in the lane for the next round. We just added
per-lane byte counts to the forge path, so the gate is a one-condition change,
and the dashboard's new %-of-byte-budget bars will show it working (EBs only
ever appear ≥ 50% of half the EB budget… displayed vs the EB budget: bars start
at ≥ 25%). Demo-wise: `Lazy Sunday` (light optimistic traffic) becomes the
scenario that shows txs *pooling* until the threshold is crossed.
Open point to raise at the weekly: is the threshold measured against the RB
byte budget (CIP framing) or against the EB's own budget?

## 2. Polina's specs — first comparison against our implementation

`ouroboros-consensus` branch `polina/mempool-spec` — three literate specs
(Praos / Leios / **Leios+pricing**). Her pricing design is close to our
prototype (two lanes, held EB, `ledger + heldEB + txs` layering, RB applied
after certified EB), with **two real divergences to flag at the weekly (P2)**:

1. **Cross-lane validation order.** Spec: the regular lane validates against
   `priorityUpdatedLedger + prior regulars` — priority txs *always* come first,
   and any priority admission cascades a regular-lane revalidation. Ours: one
   arrival-ordered view (`isLedgerState`), i.e. **first-come across lanes** —
   Nicolas's deliberate policy (conflicts must not be an attack vector; an
   admitted tx is never displaced). These give different outcomes exactly on
   cross-lane UTxO conflicts. Needs a team decision; we have live evidence for
   first-come (22/22 conflicting urgent rejected, no admitted-tx displacement).
2. **Per-lane capacities.** Spec: `prioCap` = one RB's TxMeasure, `regCap` =
   CIP-164 per-EB caps — separate budgets. Ours: both lanes currently steer
   pricing utilisation against the shared RB budget (`Bbody.hs` sets both
   capacities to `maxBytes`; the optimistic u can reach 2.0). Aligning the
   optimistic lane on the EB's own budget would change repricing behaviour —
   worth simulating before switching.

Also hers: a `heldEB` may be a *peer's* announced EB pre-validated ahead of its
certificate; ours only tracks the node's own in-flight EB. Minor for the demo,
relevant for the real mempool.

`formal-ledger-specifications` branch `polina/commutativity`
(`Reorder.lagda.md`, `StateEquiv.lagda.md`): tx reordering lemma, currently
limited to fully-independent tx lists — she says not ready. Watch item, no
prototype action; it underpins how freely a forge may reorder lanes.

## 4. Prototype finding (P4, for the weekly): full re-validation makes ingress O(backlog)

Observed live: submission throughput collapses as the mempool deepens — ~29 tx/s
at ~1k backlog, ~9 tx/s at ~4k, ~0.5 tx/s at ~15k (byte capacity NOT the cause:
25 MB override, pool at 2.1 MB). Diagnosis: our eviction fix makes every
new-block resync run FULL validation over the whole mempool
(`reapplyShelleyTx`); at 15k txs the node spends most of each inter-block
window inside that sync holding the mempool lock, so admissions starve. This is
the flip side of the trade we made to get eviction-on-price-rise: correct
evictions, linear resync cost.

**FIXED same day (direction a):** `reapplyShelleyTx` is back to the cheap
reapply plus a single O(1) re-check of the one predicate that moves under a
new tip — U1, bid vs the current quote (`recheckBidCoversQuote` in the Dijkstra
UTXO rules; `recheckTxPrice` on `ShelleyBasedEra`, re-check-nothing default,
Dijkstra override). Verified live: a storm sustained ~300 tx/s while deepening
the pool to 27k (impossible before), and the eviction wave on quote-crossing
fired intact (BidBelowQuote, traced).

**Remaining deep-backlog slowness is NOT a hidden O(n):** at ~20k+ txs the
mempool's Leios TIME-measure capacity (`mempoolTimeoutCapacity` — expected
diffusion time) saturates and admissions get `NotEnoughSpaceLeft` until the
pool drains: a bounded mempool doing backpressure, by design. Weekly question:
is that the intended bound for pricing backlogs, and should the patient lane
have its own time budget (ties into P2/P3 separate lane capacities)?

## 5. Prototype findings P4b / P4c (same day): two more admission bottlenecks, both fixed

**P4b — priority inversion through the shared diffusion-time budget.** Byte capacities were
per lane, but `mempoolTimeoutCapacity` was checked against the WHOLE pool: a deep patient
backlog saturated it and urgent admission got `NotEnoughSpaceLeft`. Fixed: per-lane time
budget. Calibration (2026-07-08): asymmetric on purpose — urgent keeps a SHORT window (1/9
of a 15 s budget ≈ 1.7 s: time-sensitive traffic must not queue for hours), the patient
lane buffers many endorser blocks' worth (8/9 ≈ 13.3 s, ~130 MB with the byte override).

**Min-fill starvation (for the weekly, observed live 2026-07-08):** a pure ≥|RB|/2 threshold
can STARVE a trickle — a handful of pooled txs below the threshold never forge, and chained
senders depending on their outputs deadlock. The rule likely needs an aging escape (issue
anyway after K rounds held); the demo works around it operationally (lane flush + feeder
auto-unwedge). Weekly framing: the lanes are separate markets
end to end — a full patient lane must never block urgent admission.

**P4c — urgent admission was O(mempool).** Every urgent admission re-applied the entire
optimistic lane and rebuilt both lanes + the txid set + the ledger tables. Measured live:
~10 tx/s urgent vs ~400 tx/s optimistic ingress at equal tx size (40x asymmetry — the
"urgent storm too weak" symptom). Under our first-come admission the cascade is provably a
no-op (input conflicts are rejected at the door, so the incoming urgent tx commutes with
every admitted tx — exactly Polina's reordering lemma). Now O(1) like the optimistic side.
**Verified live (same day):** urgent storm ingress measured at **446 tx/s accepted**
(vs 9-11 tx/s before the fix, same feeder and tx size) — the asymmetry is gone; both
lanes now admit at optimistic-class rates and the urgent quote rations the flood
(+25%/block toward the bid ceiling), which is the mechanism doing its job.

## 6. Finding (2026-07-08, first seen on the split History panels): certification pacing ALIASES the patient lane's price signal

Under a sustained fat-tx storm the patient lane saturates completely (queue pinned at its pool
cap, ~10.8k txs / 132 MB) — yet its quote never climbs: it **oscillates floor ↔ floor×1.25
(44 ↔ 55), forever**. Verified in the stream: the +25% steps land exactly on the blocks that
APPLY a certified endorser block (12 MB of usage in that block), and the rounds in between
carry ZERO optimistic usage (the next EB is still awaiting votes) → −25%. With one EB in
flight and apply-at-certification accounting, per-block utilisation alternates 1.0 / 0.0 by
construction, so the EIP-1559 controller ping-pongs and **price never rations the overload —
only pool backpressure does**.

Remedies to discuss (they differ in mechanism-philosophy):
- **Meter utilisation against per-round THROUGHPUT** (EB budget ÷ certification cycle length)
  instead of the applied block's own budget — sustained saturation then reads u≈1 every round
  and the price climbs. Cleanest signal repair.
- **Window smoothing** (already a deferred item) does NOT fix it alone: the average of 1,0,1,0
  is exactly the ½ target, so the price freezes wherever it happens to sit.
- **Accept queue-based rationing** for the patient lane (current behaviour) and treat the
  price as a floor-anchored access fee — defensible, but then the "dynamic" in the patient
  lane's dynamic pricing is mostly ornamental under load.
